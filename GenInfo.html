General Information

Asperger's is a form of Autism Spectrum Disorder(ASD) and is considered to be on the "high functioning" end of the spectrum. People with Asperger's struggle with social interactions and have a specific range of interest and often display repetitive motions.

Here are some of the behaviours often associated with Asperger's:

  • Limited social skills
  • "Robotic" or repetitive speach
  • Challenges with non verbal communication
  • Lack of eye contact
  • etc.

How is Asperger's Diagnosed?

Asperger's often remains undiagnosed untill the affected person starts struggling in school or the work place, as well as their personal lives. In many occations it is only diagnosed once the individual seeks help for other issues such as anxiety or depression. The key to being diagnosed is to look out for specific symptoms and behaviors, as listed above, in combination with each other. If you suspect someone has Asperger's Disorder it is wise to seek the help of a professional, as soon as possible.
